Sociological Imagination Vlog Builder

A guided simulator for planning a 5-minute reflective sociology vlog: choose one main topic, connect a personal example to wider society, apply at least three sociological concepts or theories, and shape a vlog plan that still leaves room for your own voice.

How this simulator works

Main aim: help you avoid a purely personal story by building a clear link between micro-level experience and macro-level society.

1. Start with biography

Choose one sociology topic and describe a personal, observed or everyday experience. Avoid sensitive details.

2. Connect to society

Identify the wider social patterns, institutions, norms, inequalities or power relations that shape that experience.

3. Build the vlog

Turn your chosen topic and micro–macro bridge into a flexible 5-minute outline. You can combine, rename, reorder or remove sections.

What sociological imagination means

Sociological imagination means looking at how personal experiences are connected to wider social forces. It asks: Is this only about me, or does it show something about society?

Micro level

Individual experience, feelings, choices, family, friends, school, work, identity and everyday life.

→

Macro level

Social class, gender, race, institutions, culture, media, policy, economy, power and inequality.

Assessment reminders built into this simulator
  • Your vlog should be about 5 minutes long.
  • Your first slide should introduce you and your topic.
  • The video should be a personal reflection linked to wider society's influences.
  • You should use relevant key terms, theories and sociological language.
  • You should include references at the end and avoid sensitive, inappropriate or copyrighted material.
